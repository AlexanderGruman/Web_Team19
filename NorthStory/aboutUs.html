NorthStory is a collaborative digital storytelling platform designed to foster social learning and support

among students who have been evacuated from their homes due to war.

It leverages the power of guided narrative creation to help students process their experiences while staying academically engaged.

By combining social interaction, learning opportunities, and emotional support, NorthStory creates a safe, vibrant, and supportive online space.

So...

What Can You Do in NorthStory?

Study Mode: Connect with peers on academic topics, exchange ideas, ask questions, and collaborate on learning projects.

Fun Mode: Take a break from academics to share light-hearted content, memes, and personal stories.

Tic Tac Toe: A simple and fun game to unwind and challenge your friends.

Quick CS Quiz: Test your computer science knowledge with interactive quiz sessions.

Future plans include adding more educational and recreational games to enhance engagement.

Join Study Groups on subjects like Math, Chemistry, and Programming.

Discuss academic challenges, solve problems together, and share study resources.

Each group is moderated to maintain a positive and collaborative atmosphere.

Every day, NorthStory displays a motivational quote to inspire positivity and resilience.

Quotes are carefully selected to resonate with the challenges students face, helping to boost morale.

A dedicated journal space allows users to anonymously express their thoughts and feelings

Promotes mental well-being by encouraging users to articulate their emotions and thoughts.

The main feed functions like a social media platform, allowing users to share stories, updates, and achievements.

Engage with peers through comments, likes, and shares, creating a sense of community and support.

NorthStory is primarily designed for students affected by war and displacement.⚔️

The platform caters to high school and university students who have been evacuated and are looking for

Social connection, Academic support and Emotional well-being❤️

Educators and mentors may also use NorthStory to support students and foster a sense of community and continuity.
